A Message from our Director
 
We are pleased to present the first issue of Inside the Science Libraries! This service will provide you with quick highlights from the Biomedical and Science and Engineering Libraries relevant to your teaching, research, and service. We will also feature services, workshops, and resources available in the libraries at UCLA.

We know access to scientific literature is crucial for your endeavors. Negotiations between the UCs and Elsevier continue. We also wanted to take this opportunity to draw attention to some of the resources and services with which you may not be familiar. Our inaugural update includes some of the ways that we are assisting our community through research support (see Systematic Review Service), incorporating scalable information literacy instruction to students (see WI+RE), using library spaces to support campus needs (see Peer-to-Peer Learning Space), and more.

If you want to learn more about any of our library services, please don't hesitate to contact your librarian. We would also love to hear what you'd like to see in future issues.

Sincerely,
Rikke Ogawa
Director of the Louise M. Darling Biomedical Library and Science and Engineering Library

 
News and Announcements


Systematic Review Service
 
Working on a systematic review? Librarians at the UCLA Biomedical Library can help you! We offer a systematic review service to research staff, graduate students, and faculty in all departments. To initiate a consultation, use our Biomedical Library Expert Search Services (BLESS) portal.
 
 
 
Library Prize for Undergraduate Research
 
Spread the word - applications are now open for the 2019 Library Prize for Undergraduate Research. The UCLA Library wants to recognize the remarkable work of undergraduate students and research teams. This prize can be a great incentive for in-class work, or highlight independent research projects. Submissions are judged by a library/faculty panel, and successful submissions will be awarded prizes ranging from $350-$700. Submissions are due by April 1, 2019.
 
 
 
Writing Instruction and Research Education (WI+RE)
 
Improve student writing and research skills with WI+RE's easy-to-implement online assignments and tools. These quick and practical learning modules include topics such as reading strategies, integrating citations, and finding scholarly articles. WI+RE materials are collaboratively designed by students, teachers, and librarians at UCLA.
 
 
 
On Display: California Rocks and Minerals Exhibit
 
Come check out the new California Rock and Mineral display at the Geology Library entrance. This collaboration with the Department of Earth, Planetary, and Space Science highlights California's exceptionally diverse geologic resources with rare samples, historic maps, and book recommendations.
 
 
New SciFinder and Reaxys Online Tutorials
 
Learn how to use our two most popular chemical data and literature databases with these two multimedia, online tutorials. Created as a joint project between the UCLA Library and UCLA Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry, and featuring Ph.D. student Robert Tobolowsky.

New Peer-to-Peer Learning Space: STEM Collaborative Learning Center
 
 
The Science and Enginnering Library, in partnership with UCLA CEILS (Center for Education Innovation and Learning in the Sciences), is now home to the STEM Collaborative Learning Center (CLC)! The STEM CLC is located in the Science and Engineering Library's Research Commons, at 8251 Boelter Hall.

The main mission is to provide a central space to foster collaborative learning among STEM majors, while introducing students to library facilities and research resources. The space is used for a combination of drop-in collaborative learning and facilitated workshops, both given by the Undergraduate Learning Assistants (LA) Program. The Learning Assistants (LA) Program is an evidence-based, multidisciplinary instructional strategy, in which students who have succeeded in a given course receive credit to learn how to help the next group of students succeed in that course. It is specifically geared toward large introductory science, engineering and math courses. Undergraduate students who serve as LAs thus help facilitate active learning and collaborative instruction in class and outside of lecture during office hours and discussion/lab sections. To learn more about the STEM CLC, contact science librarian, Courtney Hoffner.
